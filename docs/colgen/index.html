6.2.1. Strawberry Problem¶

Strawberries are growing in the cells of a rectangular field of dimensions \(A\) times \(B\). You want to build greenhouses to enclose the strawberries. Greenhouses are rectangular, axis-aligned with the field (i.e., not diagonal), and may not overlap. The cost of each greenhouse is \(C_g\) plus \(C_u\) per unit of area covered. The function \(s(i,j)\) (with \(1\leq i\leq A, j\leq y\leq B\)) is equal to \(1\) if there is a strawberry plant on square \((i,j)\).

How to cover all the strawberries at minimal cost ?

  1. Imagine a column generation approach and derive the restricted master problem.

  2. Derive the pricing problem and give an algorithm to solve it.
